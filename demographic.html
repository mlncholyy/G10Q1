Need of viewers

The reason why our viewers need to understand the impact of online gaming on high school students' social skills and interpersonal relationships is to help students between Junior and Senior High School navigate the balance between their online gaming habits and their personal growth. Our stream will provide insights into how excessive gaming can influence their ability to communicate effectively, build meaningful relationships, and develop essential social skills, both online and offline. Understanding these dynamics will allow students, parents, and educators to make informed decisions about screen time, fostering healthier social interactions that prepare them for real-world scenarios and future success in life.


Podcast

The podcast audience is evenly split between 418 males and 418 females. Most listeners are millennials, specifically those aged 25 to 34, and many of them live in urban areas like Greater Manila. When it comes to marital status, a significant majority, 85.88%, are married, while 14.12% are single. In terms of education, most listeners have completed college, with 707 holding a degree, followed by 102 who attended graduate school, 36 who finished high school, and a small number who haven’t had any formal schooling. As for employment, 72.82% of the audience are working, while 27.18% are unemployed. Overall, this podcast audience is made up of educated, working individuals who mostly come from city environments.

Webcast

The analysis shows that this webcast primarily attracts a younger audience, with respondents falling between the ages of 13 and 16, suggesting that the content is particularly appealing to high school students. The data also indicates a balanced gender representation, with three female respondents and one male respondent. All respondents are Filipino, single, and currently enrolled in high school, which highlights that the webcast is being consumed mostly by students. In terms of household income, one respondent reported a lower income bracket (below ₱10,000), while another reported a higher range (₱75,001 to ₱100,000)

Q&A

The viewers for the Q&A consist of male and female high school students between the ages of 13 and 16, all Filipino and single. Their educational attainment is at the high school level, and none are employed. While household income is generally not specified, the disclosed ranges include above ₱100,000 and between ₱50,001 and ₱75,000, offering some socioeconomic diversity. The audience represents a demographic of young, academically engaged individuals within the Filipino context.

Demographic

People from different ages, statuses, and beliefs watch streams based on a variety of factors, including the desire for personalized, interactive content, the convenience of on-demand viewing, and the appeal of niche interests or communities that resonate with their values. While younger generations, such as Gen Z, prefer highly personalized, social media-driven content that reflects their digital-native lifestyle, older generations have also adapted to streaming habits, often drawn by the flexibility and tailored experiences that fit their schedules and interests. Across all demographics, the ability to connect with content creators and communities in real-time adds a layer of engagement and loyalty that traditional media struggles to replicate.
